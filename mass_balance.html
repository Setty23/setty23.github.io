The mass balance equation, also known as the principle of mass conservation, is a fundamental concept in chemical engineering. It states that the total mass entering a system must equal the total mass leaving the system, plus any accumulation of mass within the system. Mathematically, the mass balance equation is expressed as:

Total mass in = Total mass out + Accumulation

In differential form, it can be written as:

𝑑𝑀/𝑑𝑡 = Σ𝐹in − Σ𝐹out + Σ𝐺gen

where:

  • 𝑑𝑀/𝑑𝑡 represents the rate of change of mass within the system with respect to time.
  • Σ𝐹in is the sum of all mass flows entering the system.
  • Σ𝐹out is the sum of all mass flows leaving the system.
  • Σ𝐺gen represents any generation or consumption of mass within the system.

The mass balance equation is essential for analyzing and designing chemical processes, as it ensures that mass is conserved throughout the system. It is used in various applications, including reactor design, separation processes, and environmental engineering.
